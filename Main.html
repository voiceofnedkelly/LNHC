Why is Ned Kelly seen as a hero and/or criminal?

Is Ned Kelly viewed as an anti-hero who fought against corruption or a violent criminal in today’s society?

Ned Kelly is viewed as both an anti-hero and a violent criminal depending on different people’s perspectives. To some, he was a hero, and to others, a villain. His legacy is split directly down the line of his actions.

The Case for the Hero

  • Childhood Bravery: At 10 years old, Ned Kelly was locally recognized as a hero after he courageously saved a 7-year-old boy from drowning in the rain-swollen Hughes Creek. He earned a green silk sash from the Sheltons who ran the Royal Mail Hotel. Ned treasured this sash and even wore it under his armor during the siege at Glenrowan.
  • Family Leadership: When Ned was around 11 or 12 years old (the age his father died), he became the male head of his family, shouldering immense responsibility.
  • Symbol of the Bush: In the following years, he grew to symbolize the industrious and resourceful bush settlers who survived in a harsh country. As an expert horseman, bushman, and tree feller, he came to personify the rugged Australian identity that inherently despised oppressive authority.

The Case for the Criminal

  • Outlaw Leadership: In the late 1870s, he led the Kelly Gang—a group of armed outlaws—in open defiance against the Victorian police force.
  • Violent Crimes: He evaded law enforcement, robbed major banks (such as those in Euroa and Jerilderie), shot a Constable on the wrist, killed three policemen at Stringybark Creek, and deliberately sabotaged a public railway line just before the Glenrowan shootout.
Core Summary: Many people still disagree on whether Ned Kelly should be remembered as a hero or a criminal. He is championed as a hero due to his life-saving bravery, his generosity, and his willingness to help the poor. Concurrently, he is condemned as a villain due to his severe crimes and violent actions. This debate will likely continue forever because, depending on the perspective, he genuinely embodied elements of both a hero and a criminal.

Where did Ned Kelly fight against the corrupt government?

Stringybark Creek

During the hunt for the Kelly gang, 4 policemen camped in the bush. In late 1878, Ned and Dan Kelly, with friends Joe Byrne, Steve Hart, and Tom Lloyd junior, had been hiding in the ranges above Mansfield for several months, with warrants for the arrest of the Kelly brothers for the Fitzpatrick incident in Greta. A party of 4 policemen left Mansfield on the 25th of October intending to capture the Kelly brothers. Their names were Kennedy, Lonigan, Scanlan, and McIntyre. The Kelly gang discovered the policemen’s camp and ambushed them on the following day. 3 policemen—Kennedy, Lonigan and Scanlan—were killed, with McIntyre escaping back to Mansfield, sounding the alarm. Within 4 weeks, the four were declared outlaws under an act approved by the Victorian government.

Glenrowan

On the evening of 26 June 1880, the Kelly gang prepared for a shoot-out against the police. They murdered Aaron Sherritt, who knew police would send reinforcements from Melbourne by train. They planned to derail the train and ambush the survivors. While waiting, the Kelly gang forced the townsfolk into the Glenrowan Inn. But one resident warned the police, by flagging the train, of the planned derailment. This then led to a shootout. By the end of the siege at Glenrowan, three of the Kelly gang were dead, and Ned Kelly was captured. In the end, the Inn was burned down by the police during their fight with the Kelly gang.

The Fitzpatrick incident (quick overview)

Constable Alexander Fitzpatrick arrived at Eleven Mile Creek (Kelly’s home) without a warrant to ‘arrest Dan Kelly for horse theft’. He (according to the neighbors and the Kelly family) arrived drunk and tried to kiss Kate Kelly (14 years old). This led to conflict and the constable getting shot in the wrist. This made Ned and Dan Kelly flee into the bush, and Ellen Kelly was arrested for 3 years in jail (attempted murder).

Robbery of Jerilderie bank (in Euroa)

The Kelly gang robbed the bank to distribute/support their family + friends who were poor. This included others who were in debt and poor.

Summary

Ned Kelly fought the corrupt government in many ways. The main ones include Stringybark Creek, Glenrowan, The Fitzpatrick incident, and the Robbery of the Jerilderie bank. These places were all significant battles in his journey as an outlaw.
